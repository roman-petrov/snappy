# Skill: Interior Generation

## Goal

Generate a coherent interior scene prompt with believable layout, materials, lighting, and mood.

## Use When

- User asks for room/interior visualization, concept design, mood scene, or style exploration.
- User needs one room concept or a series-ready base prompt.

## Ready For Generation Criteria

- Room type and functional goal are defined.
- Style direction and atmosphere are selected.
- Key constraints are fixed (dimensions, budget feel, occupancy use case).
- Camera viewpoint and framing intent are known.

## User Discovery Topics

- Space type, purpose, and users of the space.
- Desired style language and references.
- Color/material palette and texture preferences.
- Lighting scenario (day/night, natural/artificial, dramatic/soft).
- Furniture density, focal zones, and decluttering level.
- Practical constraints (existing elements, no-demolition limits, regional context).
- Output intent (photorealistic mood board, concept art, listing render, social post).

## Clarification Strategy

1. Minimum baseline: collect room type, purpose, style, and mood.
2. Spatial realism: clarify layout logic, key objects, lighting direction, and camera angle.
3. Final validation: confirm atmosphere, realism level, and disallowed elements.

## Quality Checklist

- Spatial composition is plausible and readable.
- Materials and lighting are internally consistent.
- Prompt includes camera/lens perspective cues.
- Atmosphere aligns with the user objective.
- Explicit exclusions prevent clutter and style drift.

## Common Failure Modes And Prevention

- Incoherent style mixing: ask for one primary style plus one controlled accent.
- Unrealistic layouts: validate movement paths and furniture scale logic.
- Flat visual mood: force lighting intent and focal hierarchy in prompt.
